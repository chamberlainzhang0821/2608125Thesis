\section{Sensitivity Analysis}
\subsection{Sensitivity Analysis of Task 1: Bayesian Fan-Vote Inference}

We test robustness of the Stable-ABC fan-vote inference to two key hyperparameters: the Dirichlet prior concentration $\alpha$
and the rejection-ABC budget \texttt{MAX\_DRAWS}. We track mean acceptance rate and mean fallback rate.

Figure~\ref{fig:sens_alpha} shows that acceptance changes only mildly over $\alpha\in[0.1,1.0]$,
while fallback quickly approaches $0$ once $\alpha\ge 0.3$, indicating stable inference under moderately informative priors.
Figure~\ref{fig:sens_budget} suggests diminishing returns from increasing \texttt{MAX\_DRAWS}, since fallback remains near $0$
and acceptance improves only marginally. Overall, Stable-ABC exhibits stable behavior under reasonable hyperparameter perturbations.

\subsection{Sensitivity Analysis of Task 3: Explanatory Regression and Sensitivity}

We assess robustness of the Task~3 ridge-regression explanations from three aspects:
(i) seed stability under different cross-validation splits,
(ii) bootstrap stability of grouped effect magnitudes,
and (iii) sensitivity to ridge regularization strength $\lambda$.

As shown in Figure~\ref{fig:t3_seed_stability_box}, the 5-fold CV $R^2$ distributions are stable across seeds,
with\\ \texttt{Judges\_AvgJudgeTotal} consistently achieving the highest explainability, followed by\\
\texttt{Performance\_WeeksSurvived}, while \texttt{Fans\_AvgFanShare} remains the most difficult target.
Figure~\ref{fig:t3_beta_ci_dotplot} further confirms that the grouped-effect ranking is robust under bootstrapping:
\texttt{Partner} is the dominant factor for judge outcomes and remains influential for fan share, while
\texttt{Industry} and geographic features contribute weaker but nonzero signals.
